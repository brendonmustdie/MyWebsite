Procedurally Generated 3D Maze:

The Development Log:

Concept:

For an assignment I created a “procedural generation algorithim” which used prefabs in unity to spawn rooms where I wanted them. I add empties in my prefabs, and in code set which opening they were in, and which rooms could therefore instantiate. I then wrote a function to randomly instantiate rooms from arrays. At the time, I was pretty happy with this idea, however I encountered some technical problems:

- Sometimes the rooms spawnned at different heights, and were tilted at strange angles.

- It wouldn’t open in a build.

Procedural generation interests me and I’m spending more time figuring out ways to do it.

Log 001 2022/05/03:

I researched Depth First Search, on the instruction of a lecturer, and I’m excited about what it could mean. If I could build a grid, and use a system like DFS, I could randomly generate a maze! And slightly altering that could make a dungeon!
